FSAE Scales Summary - May 2026

Published

2026-05-14 19:26:22

Overview

A total of 105 teams were scaled before Design. Of the cars, the lightest was 156.5kg (Vanderbilt Univ), the mean car weight was 218.9kg, and the median car was 216.8kg.

For comparison, the lightest car in 2025 was 167.8kg (University of California - Berkley), the median car was 215kg, and the average was 220.9kg.

In 2024, the lightest was 162.8kg (Vanderbilt), the median 210.9kg, and the average was 214.2kg.

In 2023, the lightest was 160.6kg (Rose-Hulman), the median 213.4kg, and the average was 216.7kg.

Photos of all cars can be found online at https://bit.ly/FSAEM26Scales

Methodology

All vehicles were weighed on Intercomp SW650 RFX scales, with the left front scale location used as common datum for all cars. The surface pad was measured within 0.1 deg of level in X, Y, and corner-to-corner, although exact pad locations for the other 3 scales varies with wheelbase and track. At last calibration (2024-04-03), all scales were certified accurate within 0.1% +/- or 1lb +/- (whichever is greater), and a spot check at site setup confirmed all scales consistent at low end (40lb) and high end (275lb) of range used on site.

Cars are weighed twice - once with the driver in the car for balance percentages (teams may select any driver, who wears a driver suit or clothing of equivalent weight and a helmet), once with the driver out (for vehicle only weight). If the driver uses a seat spacer/pad, it is removed for the car only measurement (considered driver gear). Cars are presented in dry weather configuration, and photographs of the vehicles can be checked at https://bit.ly/FSAEM26Scales
